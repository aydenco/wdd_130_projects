resources

Below is a list of various websites that you can use to learn more, find training, and build a quality first aid kit.

Founded in 1881, the Red Cross is one of the most recognized medical organizations. Their website provides lots of useful information about first aid, and you can sign up to get certified in things like CPR or BLS.

Stop the Bleed is another valuable resource for the most recent information and training in treating massive hemorrhaging.

The Committee for Tactical Emergency Casualty Care is based off of its military counterpart, CoTCCC, which is responsible for creating the MARCH Protocol. The CoTECC is geared towards first responder, search and rescue, and civillian situations.

North American Rescue is seen as the industry standard in first response medical supplies. Any quality prebuilt first aid kit will include their products. They also offer a multitude of training courses.

Sheepdog Response is a tactical training organization that is focused in self-defense and other combat skills, but they also offer an excellent trauma response course perfect for the average citizen. Their team is made up of a variety of military experts, including special ops medics. They also sell a quality IFAK and other medical supplies.

Warrior Poet Society, led by Army Ranger Veteran John Lovell, is another great option for training and medical supplies. They cater to the everyday person who may not necessarily want to spend a ton of time or money on gear and training.
